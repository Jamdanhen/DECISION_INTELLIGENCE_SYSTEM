# Principles

## Reusable Decision Framework

The system should preserve a reusable decision process that can apply across
multiple domains.

Procurement is one module, not the parent concept.

## Evidence Before Recommendation

Recommendations should be based on recorded evidence, explicit criteria, and
visible tradeoffs.

Evidence should include current market data, validated pricing, active
promotions, historical price context, product specifications, warranty terms,
upgrade requirements, and known risks.

## Scoring Is Domain-Specific

The reporting engine and decision loop should remain reusable.

Scoring models may change by domain, category, or decision type.

## Decision Records Should Be Reviewable

Decision outputs should make it clear:

- what was evaluated
- what evidence was used
- how options were scored
- what tradeoffs were accepted
- what recommendation was made
- when re-evaluation may be needed

Procurement reports should be consistent enough that future reports can be
compared against earlier reports without reconstructing the decision logic from
chat history.

## Monitoring Matters

Some decisions should remain live after the initial recommendation.

Market price, risk, lifecycle, requirements, and availability may change.

Procurement decisions should be monitored after initial evaluation. A good
recommendation can expire when pricing, promotions, availability, requirements,
or evidence changes.

## Repository First

The repository is the durable asset.

Prompts, chats, and interim conversations may help produce work, but durable
behavior should be captured in project files, standards, templates, evidence
records, reports, and monitoring notes.

## Workflow Fit Over Lowest Price

Technology procurement should optimize for ready-to-use productivity, workflow
fit, total ownership value, wireless performance, reliability, and reasonable
future flexibility.

Lowest purchase price alone is not the goal.

## Complete Cost Matters

Procurement comparisons should use completed system cost.

Completed cost includes required upgrades, accessories, shipping, taxes,
warranty or support additions when relevant, and the time or difficulty needed
to reach productive use.

## Budget Discipline With Explicit Exceptions

The search should not balloon into a money sink simply because higher-priced
options exist.

For the primary workstation case, preserve a target price, a normal hard target,
and an exceptional candidate band.

Exceptional candidates may be shown above the normal hard target only when the
incremental value is explicit and material.

They should not silently become the new baseline.

## Preferred Storage Is Not Always A Gate

For workstation procurement, 2 TB NVMe storage is preferred but not always a
day-one requirement.

1 TB NVMe storage is acceptable at purchase when the system has a clear,
low-risk path to 2 TB or more.

512 GB storage is too tight for the primary workstation unless explicitly
temporary.

## Reliability Has A Planning Horizon

The primary development workstation should be expected to remain fast and stable
for 4-5 years.

The minimum acceptable expected useful life is 3 years.

Do not overbuy solely to chase a 7-10 year lifespan when flexibility, upgrade
paths, and future market changes may create better value.

## Wireless Is A First-Class Requirement

For workstation procurement, wireless quality is not incidental.

Evaluate Wi-Fi version, chipset, antenna quality, reliability, real-world
performance evidence, and upgradeability.

## Authority Boundaries

The system supports decisions.

It does not automatically own the projects, repositories, purchases, or
policies affected by those decisions.
