# Workstation Scoring Draft

Status:

```text
Working draft
```

This draft supports the Primary Development Workstation case. It is not a final
reusable scoring standard.

## Mandatory Gates

A candidate should be rejected or treated as a special exception if it cannot
meet these gates within the normal hard target.

- Completed cost at or below $1,500 for normal recommendations
- Completed cost above $1,500 and up to $2,000 only for exceptional candidates
  with explicit incremental value
- Windows 11 Pro available or included
- 32 GB RAM included for practical normal recommendations
- 16 GB RAM only as a value-build exception when completed upgrade cost is
  validated and materially cheaper than comparable 32 GB systems
- 64 GB RAM included or upgradeable as a preference, not a day-one gate for the
  recalibrated practical lane
- At least 1 TB NVMe storage included
- Clear, low-risk upgrade path to 2 TB or more
- Intel Core Ultra 5, Intel Core i5 or better, AMD Ryzen 5 or better
- Wi-Fi 6E minimum, unless wired-first use is explicitly accepted
- Reasonable path to maintenance, support, or return
- Expected useful life of at least 3 years

## Preferred Targets

Preferred completed system:

- Approximately $1,000 completed cost
- Practical target range of $800-$1,300
- Normal hard target of $1,500
- Exceptional candidate visibility up to $2,000 when materially justified
- 32 GB RAM ready-to-use
- 64 GB RAM preferred when it does not distort completed cost
- 2 TB NVMe preferred, but not mandatory when 1 TB expansion is easy
- 1 TB NVMe acceptable at purchase when expansion to 2 TB or more is easy and
  low-risk
- Wi-Fi 7
- Excellent real-world wireless evidence
- Windows 11 Pro included
- Easy maintenance
- Expected to remain a fast, stable primary development workstation for 4-5
  years

## Recalibrated Practical Lane

For the current Primary Development Workstation case, the practical lane should
lead unless the user explicitly chooses an exceptional spend path.

Practical-lane candidates should generally be:

- $800-$1,300 before tax, with completed cost still disciplined
- Windows 11 Pro
- 32 GB RAM included
- 1 TB or 2 TB NVMe included
- RAM/storage upgradeable without unusual risk
- sold by a direct or trusted seller
- strong enough for ChatGPT, Codex, VS Code, GitHub, Markdown repositories,
  Microsoft Office, browser-heavy research, and documentation
- stable enough to act as a repository-centered work hub across locations

Do not treat 96 GB RAM, newest AI CPU branding, or above-$1,500 pricing as the
default workstation standard.

Current RAM-price caution:

- Current DDR5 SODIMM upgrade prices may be high enough to make immediate
  buy-low-upgrade paths unattractive.
- A 16 GB system should not be recommended over a 32 GB system unless the
  completed upgrade cost is actually verified and materially lower.

## Work Hub Fit

The current workstation should be evaluated as a work hub, not only as a local
desktop.

Hub-fit criteria:

- stable repository storage
- easy backup and sync strategy
- reliable Ethernet and Wi-Fi
- low-friction remote or multi-location access
- predictable sleep/wake or always-available behavior
- low idle power
- quiet operation
- enough storage for active work without constant cleanup
- clear recovery path if the device fails

For hub use, 2 TB storage has more value than extra CPU performance. A 1 TB
candidate can still win if expansion and backup are simple.

Current baseline note:

- Sleeper, the user's current laptop, has a 12th Gen Intel Core i7-1260P,
  16 GB RAM, and 954 GB storage with 171 GB used.
- The target upgrade should not assume a storage-starved or obsolete baseline.
- 32 GB RAM is the clearest practical improvement.
- 1 TB storage remains acceptable when GitHub and backup/sync are sound.
- 2 TB storage is convenience and headroom, not a hard need.

## Working Weights

Use a 100-point working score after mandatory gates.

- Ready-to-use value: 20
- Workflow fit: 15
- Repository workstation and hub suitability: 18
- Network and wireless stability: 14
- Reliability, support, and 4-5 year useful-life fit: 10
- Backup, sync, and recovery fit: 8
- Total ownership value: 12
- Performance: 4
- Expandability and maintenance: 5
- Warranty and return path: 3

## Risk Flags

Risk flags should be visible beside the score.

- Soldered memory below preferred capacity
- Unknown or weak wireless chipset evidence
- Weak Ethernet or hub networking evidence
- Unclear backup/sync/recovery path
- Sleep/wake or always-on reliability concerns
- Consumer vendor support risk
- Upgrade required before productive use
- 16 GB starting RAM during high RAM-price conditions
- 512 GB storage
- 1 TB storage with unclear expansion path
- No meaningful improvement over Sleeper for the intended hub role
- Expected useful life below 4 years
- Refurbished or third-party upgraded configuration
- Price source not directly from vendor or major retailer
- Completed cost above $1,500
- Weak justification for exceptional-candidate pricing
- Historical pricing unknown
- Return or warranty terms unclear

## Purchase Readiness

Purchase Readiness Score is separate from product quality.

It should reflect:

- Evidence completeness
- Price confidence
- Promotion confidence
- Requirements fit
- Risk clarity
- Availability
- Time to productivity
