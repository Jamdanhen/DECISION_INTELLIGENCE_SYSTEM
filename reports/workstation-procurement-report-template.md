# Workstation Procurement Report Template

Status:

```text
Reusable report template for the Primary Development Workstation case
```

## 1. Executive Summary

Summarize recommendation posture, leading candidates, major risks, and whether
the current state is Buy Now or Wait.

## 2. Purchase Readiness Score

Score from 0 to 100.

Explain what evidence is complete and what evidence remains missing.

## 3. Current Market Summary

Summarize market conditions, pricing movement, availability, and candidate
lanes.

## 4. Current Price Snapshot

For every candidate include:

- clickable product link built into the candidate name
- current price
- typical price
- historical low
- current promotion
- coupon availability
- ready-to-use status
- required upgrades
- estimated completed cost
- time to productivity
- budget band: target, normal hard target, exceptional candidate, or out of
  scope

## 5. Top 10 Overall Recommendations

Rank the current field and explain why each candidate sits where it does.

Candidate names should be clickable product links wherever a product page is
known.

## 6. Category Rankings

Include:

- Top 3 Ready-to-Use
- Top 3 Upgrade Candidates
- Top 3 Mini PCs
- Top 3 Desktops / Workstations

## 7. Category Winners

Name provisional or final winners by category.

## 8. Active Promotions

Record visible promotions, coupon codes, expiration dates, and confidence.

## 9. Historical Pricing

Record typical price, historical low, and pricing trend where available.

If unavailable, mark unknown.

## 10. Incremental Value Analysis

Explain what extra money buys or fails to buy across the leading options.

Use completed cost, not base price alone.

Apply the storage rule:

- 512 GB is too tight unless explicitly temporary.
- 1 TB is acceptable at purchase if expansion to 2 TB or more is clear and
  low-risk.
- 2 TB is the preferred comfort target.

Apply the reliability horizon:

- Minimum acceptable expected useful life is 3 years.
- Target expected useful life is 4-5 years.
- 5+ years is a bonus, not a reason to overbuy by itself.

Apply the budget band rule:

- Around $1,000 is the preferred target.
- $1,500 is the normal hard target for recommendations.
- $1,500-$2,000 candidates may be shown only as explicit exceptional candidates
  with clear incremental value.
- Above $2,000 is out of scope unless the user changes the budget.

## 11. Procurement Decision

End every report with:

- Buy Now or Wait
- Why
- Confidence
- Rationale
