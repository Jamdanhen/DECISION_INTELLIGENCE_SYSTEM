## 3. Current Market Summary

Current market structure:

- Under $1,000: mostly value or upgrade-build candidates.
- $1,000-$1,500: some strong candidates, but 64 GB RAM and Windows 11 Pro remain
  hard to satisfy cleanly without compromises.
- $1,500-$2,000: legitimate exceptional candidates appear, especially AI mini
  PCs with stronger platforms and better ready-to-use posture.

The procurement issue is not whether good machines exist. They do.

The issue is whether any candidate clearly meets the normal recommendation band
without hidden upgrade, stock, warranty, or configuration risk.

## 10. Incremental Value Analysis

The most important current tradeoff is between waiting for a normal-budget MS-A2
configuration and considering the AI X1 Pro-370 as an exceptional candidate.

MS-A2 remains more aligned with budget discipline, but it is not target-ready
today from the official store. Buying the 32 GB / 1 TB model and upgrading RAM
would likely push completed cost near or above the normal hard target.

AI X1 Pro-370 is the cleanest visible exceptional candidate today. Its 64 GB /
1 TB price of $1,509 exceeds the normal hard target by only $9 before taxes and
shipping. Its 96 GB / 2 TB cart total of $1,953 after coupon is inside the
exceptional visibility band before taxes and shipping, and even with optional
shipping protection it remains under $2,000 before taxes/shipping. The premium
buys Wi-Fi 7, Ryzen AI HX 370, OCuLink, dual USB4, triple M.2 storage,
fingerprint, and a ready-to-use high-memory/high-storage configuration.

GEEKOM A9 Max remains competitive, but exact configuration matters. If the
useful configuration is $1,399 with only 32 GB RAM, it is weaker than it first
appears. If a 64 GB configuration is available within the exceptional band, it
could compete directly with the AI X1 Pro-470.

GEEKOM IT13 Max AI is the best new value-build candidate. At $899, it has a
strong platform, Wi-Fi 7, dual 2.5G Ethernet, free US shipping, 3-year warranty,
and a real upgrade path to 96 GB RAM and up to 6 TB storage. Its limitation is
the starting 16 GB RAM. It does not beat the Pro-370 as ready-to-use, but it may
beat lower-cost barebones if the RAM upgrade path is economical.

GEEKOM A7 Max and GMKtec M6 Ultra are not dead, but current RAM/storage pricing
means they should be treated as value-build candidates, not automatic value
winners.
