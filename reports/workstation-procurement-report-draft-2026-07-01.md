## 3. Current Market Summary

The current market appears to split into three useful lanes:

- Value mini PCs: low starting price, strong CPUs, and upgradeable RAM/storage,
  but weaker business support and more configuration verification required.
- Business mini desktops: better support, warranty, Windows 11 Pro alignment,
  and enterprise reliability posture, but often higher cost.
- Mini workstations: stronger expansion, networking, and storage paths, but may
  be noisier, less ready-to-use, or close to the hard ceiling after upgrades.

The best next move is to validate exact completed cost for the leading
candidates instead of expanding the field too broadly.

## 10. Incremental Value Analysis

Early value tradeoffs:

- GMKtec NucBox M6 Ultra may offer the lowest completed cost, but could require
  more setup and has weaker support posture than business desktops.
- GEEKOM A7 Max appears to offer a strong balance of CPU, Windows 11 Pro,
  warranty posture, Wi-Fi 7 evidence, and upgradeability.
- GEEKOM A9 Max provides a stronger AI/performance lane, but the visible price
  leaves less room for RAM upgrades under the hard ceiling.
- HP EliteDesk 8 Mini G1a may be worth paying more for if warranty, business
  support, and reliability are prioritized over lowest completed cost.
- MINISFORUM MS-A2 may offer excellent expandability, but setup effort,
  thermals, noise, and support risk need deeper review.
- MINISFORUM MS-01 has compelling networking and storage expansion, but the
  price-to-completed-cost path may be too close to the hard ceiling.

## 11. Procurement Decision

Decision:

```text
Wait
```

Why:

The visible candidates are now concrete enough to inspect, but not verified
enough to buy. The next procurement step should validate exact completed cost,
upgrade path, Wi-Fi chipset, warranty, return terms, and historical price for
the top candidates.

Confidence:

```text
Medium confidence in the candidate field.
Low confidence in purchase timing.
```

Rationale:

The project criteria are clear, but price and promotion evidence is incomplete.
Buying now would risk selecting a machine before confirming whether a better
candidate meets the 64 GB / 2 TB / Windows 11 Pro / wireless requirements under
the $1,500 hard ceiling.
