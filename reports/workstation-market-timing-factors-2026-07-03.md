## Factors Added

Three market timing factors now affect workstation procurement posture:

1. AI-driven pressure on chip, memory, and SSD supply.
2. Pending back-to-school sales.
3. User's preferred purchase horizon: sooner rather than later, with an outside
   window of about one month.

## Factor 1: AI-Driven Component Pressure

Current market evidence supports treating memory and SSD pricing as an upward
risk through 2026.

Practical meaning for this workstation case:

- A ready-to-use 32 GB / 2 TB configuration has more value than a cheap base
  system that needs RAM or storage upgrades.
- RAM and SSD upgrade plans are riskier than they look because upgrade parts may
  get more expensive or become less consistently available.
- A candidate with 16 GB RAM should not lead unless the completed 32 GB cost is
  real and materially cheaper.
- A clean 32 GB / 2 TB sale should not be ignored merely because a theoretical
  future sale might be lower.

## Factor 2: Back-To-School Sales

Back-to-school sales create a near-term reason not to panic-buy an ordinary
markdown.

Practical meaning for this workstation case:

- July and August sale windows may produce better computer discounts.
- Retailers may use back-to-school promos to clear existing configurations
  before component-cost pressure shows up in newer stock.
- Sale labels are not enough; cart-confirmed price matters.
- The strongest buying window may be from now through the back-to-school sale
  period, not necessarily Black Friday.

## Combined Timing Rule

The two factors create a balanced rule:

```text
Watch aggressively, but do not wait through a genuinely strong sale on a ready
32 GB / 2 TB configuration.
```

The user's purchase horizon sharpens that rule:

```text
Aim to reach a buy/no-buy decision within the next month at the outside end.
```

For the GEEKOM IT15 32 GB / 2 TB:

- $1,499 is not urgent.
- $1,274 is a good sale if cart-confirmed.
- $1,199 or lower is a strong buy.
- $1,099 or lower is an exceptional buy.
- $999 or lower is historic-low-zone pricing and should be treated as a
  same-day decision point.

## Procurement Implication

The best current posture is:

```text
Wait-watch, not passive wait.
```

This means:

- Continue scanning Amazon, Best Buy, and direct GEEKOM pricing.
- Continue watching pending back-to-school promotions.
- Prefer completed configurations over upgrade-dependent bargains.
- Reassess immediately if a ready 32 GB / 2 TB candidate crosses the strong-buy
  threshold.
- Do not stretch the decision indefinitely waiting for a theoretical historic
  low if the candidate is strong, ready-to-use, and priced inside the good sale
  zone.
