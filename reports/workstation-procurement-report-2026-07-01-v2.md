## 1. Executive Summary

The workstation search is now past the loose shortlist stage.

The current evidence points to a clearer procurement posture:

- Wait before buying.
- Do not assume that a cheap mini PC plus upgrades is currently the best value.
- Current high DDR5 SODIMM and NVMe pricing makes completed cost the central
  issue.
- Keep the preferred target around $1,000 and the normal hard target at $1,500.
  Show exceptional candidates up to $2,000 only when their added value is clear.
- Prioritize candidates that already include Windows 11 Pro, 64 GB RAM or a
  strong RAM path, and at least 1 TB NVMe with a clear low-risk path to 2 TB or
  more.
- 2 TB storage remains preferred, but 1 TB is acceptable at purchase when
  expansion is straightforward.
- Reliability should be evaluated against a 3-year minimum and a 4-5 year target
  useful life, not a 7-10 year overbuy horizon.

Current strongest candidates to keep under active review:

1. MINISFORUM MS-A2, if a 64 GB / 2 TB Windows 11 Pro configuration can be
   validated at or below $1,500.
2. GEEKOM A9 Max, if 32 GB RAM is acceptable temporarily or a 64 GB path can be
   validated under the ceiling.
3. GEEKOM A7 Max, only if a preconfigured or discounted 64 GB / 2 TB path exists.
4. MINISFORUM AI X1 Pro-470, as an exceptional/show candidate if a 64 GB
   configuration stays close enough to the upper visibility band.
5. HP EliteDesk 8 Mini G1a, as the business-grade benchmark, but likely above
   budget in target configuration.

The likely rejection pattern:

- GMKtec M6 Ultra remains a strong low-cost baseline, but current RAM/SSD/OS
  upgrade costs weaken it for the 64 GB / 2 TB target.
- Dell, HP, Lenovo, and ASUS business micro desktops are attractive reliability
  benchmarks, but target configurations appear likely to exceed budget unless a
  promotion appears.

## 3. Current Market Summary

The market is currently unfavorable for upgrade-heavy paths.

Mini PCs remain attractive because base prices are low, but the workstation
criteria are not base-price criteria. The target is completed productivity:
Windows 11 Pro, 64 GB RAM or a credible path to it, at least 1 TB NVMe with a
clear path to 2 TB or more, strong wireless, reliability, and easy maintenance.

Current market observations:

- DDR5 SODIMM upgrade pricing is high enough to materially change the value
  equation.
- 2 TB NVMe pricing is also elevated compared with prior lows.
- Business-grade mini desktops often include better support and warranty paths,
  but their 64 GB / 2 TB configurations tend to exceed the hard ceiling.
- Premium AI mini PCs now offer strong performance and Wi-Fi 7, but many ship
  with 32 GB RAM even when storage is acceptable.
- Mini workstations offer the best expandability, but they introduce setup,
  thermal, noise, and support questions.

## 10. Incremental Value Analysis

The biggest procurement insight is that upgrade cost changes the rankings.

GEEKOM A7 Max and GMKtec M6 Ultra look attractive at base price, but reaching
64 GB / 2 TB may add hundreds of dollars, and in barebone cases Windows 11 Pro
can add another $199.99 if sourced directly from Microsoft.

GEEKOM A9 Max costs more up front, but it includes much more of the desired
machine: Windows 11 Pro, Wi-Fi 7, strong CPU, strong ports, and acceptable
storage paths. Its problem is RAM: 32 GB may be operationally acceptable for a
while, but it misses the preferred 64 GB target.

MINISFORUM AI X1 Pro-470 changes the stretch conversation. The 64 GB / 1 TB
configuration at $1,639 is above the normal hard target but inside the
exceptional visibility band. The added value is Wi-Fi 7, stronger AI-oriented
platform, OCuLink, dual USB4, three M.2 slots, fingerprint, and a cleaner
ready-to-use path than many upgrade builds. It should be shown, but not allowed
to redefine the budget baseline. The 96 GB / 2 TB configuration at $2,039 is
outside the current visibility band unless discounted.

HP EliteDesk 8 Mini G1a is the reliability/support model. It is likely the
calmest purchase if budget expands, but under the current ceiling it is not yet
the value winner.

MINISFORUM MS-A2 is the most interesting "nuts to butts" candidate because it
can plausibly meet the workstation role with strong CPU, 64 GB memory paths,
Windows 11 Pro evidence, Wi-Fi 6E, and expansion. With the clarified storage
rule, a 64 GB / 1 TB configuration may be acceptable if the storage expansion
path is easy and low-risk. The unresolved question is exact target configuration
price and support risk.

MINISFORUM MS-01 has excellent networking and storage expansion, but it is less
clearly aligned with the actual workflow unless homelab/server-like expansion
matters. It may be more machine shape than the workstation decision needs.

## 11. Procurement Decision

Decision:

```text
Wait
```

Why:

No candidate has yet been fully validated as meeting Windows 11 Pro, 64 GB RAM
or a clearly acceptable RAM path, at least 1 TB NVMe with low-risk expansion to
2 TB or more, Wi-Fi 6E or better, acceptable reliability/support, 3-year minimum
useful life, 4-5 year target fit, and completed cost under $1,500.

Confidence:

```text
High confidence that buying today is premature.
Medium confidence that the top active lead is MINISFORUM MS-A2.
Medium confidence that GEEKOM A9 Max is the best polished mini PC lead.
```

Rationale:

The project should now narrow rather than broaden. The next verification pass
should focus on exact checkout-level target configurations for:

1. MINISFORUM MS-A2 64 GB / 1 TB or 64 GB / 2 TB Windows 11 Pro, with storage
   expansion path verified
2. MINISFORUM AI X1 Pro-470 64 GB / 1 TB exceptional-candidate value check
3. GEEKOM A9 Max HX370 or HX470 configuration and RAM path
4. HP EliteDesk 8 Mini G1a 64 GB / 2 TB direct HP price
5. GEEKOM A7 Max 64 GB / 2 TB availability or upgrade economics

If MINISFORUM MS-A2 validates under $1,500 with acceptable warranty/return
terms, 64 GB RAM, at least 1 TB storage, and easy expansion to 2 TB or more, it
becomes the leading practical recommendation. If it fails on support, noise, or
exact cost, GEEKOM A9 Max becomes the leading polished option, with the explicit
tradeoff that 32 GB RAM may be accepted temporarily or upgraded later.
